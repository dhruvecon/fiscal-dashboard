Note on FY 2025-26 (March 2026): The war began on 28 February 2026. The March impact falls on FY 2025-26: extra expenditure Rs 6,437 crore (fertiliser Rs 3,994 Cr + LPG Rs 2,443 Cr), net revenue gain Rs 259 crore (customs +Rs 1,868 Cr, dividend −Rs 1,609 Cr; no SAED cut in March). Net extra fiscal deficit: Rs 6,178 crore, pushing FY 2025-26 FD from 4.40% (RE) to 4.42% (+0.02pp). This is excluded from the FY 2026-27 calculations shown above.
Methodology & Key Assumptions
Estimation approach: Bottom-up, channel-by-channel. Every number is either sourced from official data (PPAC, PIB, Union Budget SBE/FRBM) or flagged as an author assumption. No econometric estimation — this is a mechanical cost-pass-through model.

Price shock only, not quantity shock (except for LPG): For fertiliser, the government has committed to ensuring full availability as demanded (PIB, 24 Mar 2026). For crude oil, India's Hormuz exposure (~40–50% of imports) is being partially offset by alternative sourcing from non-Gulf producers. We therefore model only the price increase, not supply disruption-driven rationing. This makes our estimates conservative.

Fertiliser — three channels: (A) Costlier domestic urea production: gas blend shifts from 13/87 domestic/import to 28/72 (Gas Control Order, 9 Mar); spot LNG rises from $11.47 to $22–28/mmbtu; production capped at 67,000 MT/day. (B) Costlier urea imports: requirement 375 LMT vs 205 LMT war production → ~277 LMT imports at spot $700–770 vs pre-war $472/MT. (C) Costlier DAP imports: 53% imported, spot rises from $626.5 to $670–700/MT. Gas efficiency: 30 mmbtu/MT.

LPG: OMC cost scaled with Saudi Aramco CP from PIB baseline (Rs 987 at $540/MT). Under-recovery = OMC cost − retail price (Rs 913 non-PMUY, Rs 613 PMUY). 75:25 govt–OMC split (FY24-25 precedent). War consumption 2,300 TMT/month (demand management).

Oil revenue: SAED cut Rs 10/L on both petrol and diesel (Gazette, 26 Mar 2026). Customs gain: 2.5% BCD × Δcrude × monthly imports (PPAC). Dividend foregone: OMCs in loss → no dividends (Rs 1,609 Cr/month baseline). Pump prices frozen; Rs 2/month assumed increase from May.
